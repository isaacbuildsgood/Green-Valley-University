Course Overview

The PhD in English Literature is a rigorous research program designed for scholars seeking to make original contributions to the field. Students conduct advanced research, develop expertise in their chosen area, and produce a substantial dissertation under faculty mentorship.

The program's purpose is to cultivate independent researchers and scholars capable of advancing knowledge in English Literature through original research, publication, and academic leadership.

Learning Outcomes

🔬

Conduct original, publishable research in literary studies

📚

Demonstrate mastery of literary theory and methodology

🎓

Develop advanced teaching and mentoring skills

💡

Contribute new insights to literary scholarship

📝

Produce a substantial dissertation of scholarly significance

🌐

Engage with the broader academic community through conferences and publications

Course Structure

Years 1–2 — Coursework & Exams
Seminars, methodology, and comprehensive assessments
  • Advanced Seminars in Literary Theory
  • Research Methodology
  • Specialized Area Studies
  • Teaching Practicum
  • Comprehensive Examinations
Years 3–4 — Dissertation Research
Focused independent research and writing
  • Dissertation Proposal Development
  • Independent Research
  • Dissertation Writing
  • Regular Progress Reviews
  • Conference Presentations
Years 5–6 — Completion & Defense
Finalization, defense, and publication preparation
  • Final Dissertation Writing
  • Oral Defense Preparation
  • Dissertation Defense
  • Publication Preparation
  • Job Market Preparation

Career Opportunities

Academic Positions

Tenure-track faculty, research universities, liberal arts colleges

Research Institutions

Research fellowships, think tanks, cultural institutions

Publishing and Media

Senior editor, literary director, cultural critic

Policy and Consulting

Cultural policy advisor, educational consultant

Further Study Options

PhD graduates may pursue postdoctoral research or advanced academic positions.

Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • Master's degree in English Literature or related field
  • Minimum GPA of 3.7 (or equivalent)
  • Strong research and writing portfolio

Language Requirements

  • TOEFL iBT: 100 or IELTS: 7.5 (for non-native English speakers)
  • English proficiency may be demonstrated through other standardized tests

Additional requirements may apply for international students. Contact admissions for detailed information.
