Course Overview

Comprehensive dental training including clinical practice and patient care.

Learning Outcomes

🦷

Develop clinical skills for patient diagnosis and care

🔬

Apply evidence-based dental treatments and protocols

📋

Manage clinical practice and patient communication

Course Structure

Year 1 — Foundations
Biomedical sciences fundamentals
  • Human Anatomy & Physiology
  • Dental Materials
  • Preclinical Laboratory
Year 2 — Preclinical Skills
Basic clinical skills and techniques
  • Dental Pathology
  • Basic Clinical Skills
  • Operative Dentistry
Year 3 — Clinical Practice
Patient care and clinical rotations
  • Clinical Rotations
  • Patient Management
  • Specialised Dentistry
Year 4 — Advanced Clinical
Advanced procedures and complex cases
  • Advanced Clinical Cases
  • Restorative & Prosthodontics
  • Oral Surgery
Year 5 — Capstone & Professional
Final project and professional preparation
  • Final Clinical Project
  • Professional Development
  • Leadership in Dentistry

Career Opportunities

Clinical Practice

General dentist, dental surgeon

Hospital & Community

Community dental health, hospital dentistry

Research & Academia

Dental researcher, academic roles

Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • High school diploma with strong science background
  • Minimum GPA of 3.2 (or equivalent)

Language Requirements

  • TOEFL iBT: 90 or IELTS: 7.0 (for non-native English speakers)
