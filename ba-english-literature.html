Course Overview

The Bachelor of Arts in English Literature provides a comprehensive exploration of literary works from various periods, cultures, and genres. Students develop critical thinking, analytical skills, and a deep appreciation for literature while engaging in creative writing and research.

The program's purpose is to equip students with the knowledge and skills necessary to analyze texts, understand cultural contexts, and communicate effectively through written and oral expression. It fosters intellectual curiosity and prepares graduates for diverse careers in education, publishing, media, and beyond.

Learning Outcomes

📖

Analyze and interpret literary texts from diverse historical and cultural contexts

✍️

Develop advanced writing and communication skills across various genres

🔍

Conduct independent research and apply critical thinking to complex problems

🌍

Understand and appreciate global literary traditions and cultural diversity

🎭

Engage with creative processes and artistic expression

💼

Prepare for professional careers requiring strong analytical and communication skills

Course Structure

Year 1 — Foundations
Core skills and introductory surveys
  • Introduction to Literary Studies
  • English Grammar and Composition
  • World Literature Survey
  • Creative Writing Basics
  • Academic Writing and Research
Year 2 — Analysis & Genres
Develop analytical strategies and explore genres
  • Shakespeare and Renaissance Drama
  • Victorian Literature
  • American Literature
  • Poetry Workshop
  • Literary Criticism
Year 3 — Advanced Studies
Specializations and elective study
  • Modernist Literature
  • Postcolonial Literature
  • Advanced Creative Writing
  • Literary Theory
  • Elective: Contemporary Fiction, Poetry, or Drama
Year 4 — Capstone & Research
Independent project and professional preparation
  • Senior Thesis/Project
  • Internship or Practicum
  • Advanced Literary Seminar
  • Professional Development
  • Portfolio Preparation

Career Opportunities

Education

Teaching at secondary or tertiary levels, educational administration

Publishing

Editor, literary agent, content creator, publishing assistant

Media and Communications

Journalist, copywriter, content strategist, social media manager

Creative Industries

Author, screenwriter, playwright, arts administrator

Further Study Options

Graduates may pursue Master's or PhD programs in English Literature, Creative Writing, Education, Law, or related fields.

Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 (or equivalent)
  • Strong background in English language and literature

Language Requirements

  • TOEFL iBT: 80 or IELTS: 6.5 (for non-native English speakers)
  • English proficiency may be demonstrated through other standardized tests

Additional requirements may apply for international students. Contact admissions for detailed information.
