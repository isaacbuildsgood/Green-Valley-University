Bachelor of Laws (International Law)

Undergraduate Degree

4 Years

Department of International Law

Apply Now

Course Overview

Legal education with emphasis on international law, treaties, and comparative legal systems.

Learning Outcomes

⚖️

Analyse international treaties and comparative legal systems

🧭

Apply international legal principles to transboundary issues

💬

Communicate complex legal analysis clearly and persuasively

Course Structure

Year 1 — Foundations
Legal fundamentals and international law principles
  • Introduction to Law
  • Public International Law
  • Legal Research & Writing
Year 2 — Core Legal Topics
Comparative and human rights law
  • Comparative Law
  • International Human Rights
  • International Treaties
Year 3 — Applied Learning
Practical training and case work
  • Moot Court
  • Legal Clinic
  • Elective Modules
Year 4 — Capstone & Specialisation
Final projects and professional practice
  • Capstone Research Paper
  • Advanced Seminars
  • Professional Development

Career Opportunities

Legal Practice

Lawyer, legal advisor (international)

International Organisations

Roles in UN, NGOs, intergovernmental bodies

Policy & Academia

Policy analyst, researcher

Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 (or equivalent)

Language Requirements

  • TOEFL iBT: 80 or IELTS: 6.5 (for non-native English speakers)
