Course Overview

The PhD in Theatre Arts is a rigorous research and creative program designed for scholars and artists seeking to make original contributions to theatrical knowledge and practice. Students conduct advanced research, develop expertise in their chosen area, and produce a substantial dissertation or creative thesis under faculty mentorship.

The program's purpose is to cultivate independent researchers and artists capable of advancing knowledge in theatre through original research, creative work, publication, and academic and artistic leadership.

Learning Outcomes

🎭

Conduct original, publishable research and creative work in theatre

📚

Demonstrate mastery of theatre history, theory, and practice

🎓

Develop advanced teaching, mentoring, and artistic leadership skills

💡

Contribute new insights to theatre scholarship and artistic practice

📝

Produce a substantial dissertation or creative thesis of scholarly significance

🌐

Engage with the broader academic and artistic community through conferences and presentations

Course Structure

Years 1–2 — Coursework & Preparation
Seminars, methodology, and comprehensive assessments
  • Advanced Seminars in Theatre History and Theory
  • Research Methodology in Theatre
  • Specialized Study in Theatre Disciplines
  • Teaching Practicum
  • Comprehensive Examinations
Years 3–4 — Research and Creative Work
Focused independent research, creative practice, and writing
  • Dissertation Proposal Development
  • Independent Research and Creative Project
  • Dissertation or Creative Thesis Writing
  • Regular Progress Reviews
  • Conference Presentations
Years 5–6 — Completion & Defense
Finalization, defense, and professional preparation
  • Final Dissertation/Thesis Writing
  • Oral Defense Preparation
  • Dissertation Defense
  • Publication Preparation
  • Job Market Preparation

Career Opportunities

Academic Positions

Tenure-track faculty, research universities, liberal arts colleges

Professional Theatre

Artistic director, research-based theatre, experimental performance

Research Institutions

Research fellowships, cultural institutions, theatre archives

Leadership Roles

Theatre company leadership, cultural policy, educational administration

Further Study Options

PhD graduates may pursue postdoctoral research or advanced academic and artistic positions.

Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • Master's degree in Theatre Arts or related field
  • Minimum GPA of 3.7 (or equivalent)
  • Strong research, creative, and writing portfolio

Language Requirements

  • TOEFL iBT: 100 or IELTS: 7.5 (for non-native English speakers)
  • English proficiency may be demonstrated through other standardized tests

Additional requirements may apply for international students. Contact admissions for detailed information.
