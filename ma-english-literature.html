Course Overview

The Master of Arts in English Literature offers advanced study in literary theory, criticism, and research. Students engage deeply with canonical and contemporary texts, developing expertise in specialized areas while honing their analytical and scholarly skills.

The program's purpose is to prepare students for advanced academic and professional careers in literature, research, and related fields. It emphasizes independent research, critical discourse, and the application of theoretical frameworks to literary analysis.

Learning Outcomes

📖

Conduct advanced literary analysis and theoretical interpretation

🔬

Design and execute independent research projects

💬

Engage in scholarly discourse and academic writing

🎓

Develop expertise in specialized literary periods or genres

📝

Produce publishable-quality research and criticism

🏫

Prepare for doctoral studies or academic teaching positions

Course Structure

Year 1 — Core & Research Methods
Advanced coursework and research foundations
  • Advanced Literary Theory
  • Research Methods in Literary Studies
  • Seminar in Literary Criticism
  • Specialized Reading Course
  • Academic Writing Workshop
Year 2 — Specialization & Thesis
Focused research and thesis preparation
  • Elective Seminars (choose 2)
  • Independent Study
  • Thesis Research and Writing
  • Oral Defense Preparation
  • Professional Development Seminar

Career Opportunities

Academic Careers

University teaching, research positions, academic administration

Publishing and Media

Editor, literary critic, content strategist, media analyst

Research and Consulting

Literary researcher, cultural consultant, policy advisor

Creative Industries

Author, screenwriter, arts program director

Further Study Options

Graduates may pursue PhD programs in English Literature or related disciplines.

Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in English Literature or related field
  • Minimum GPA of 3.5 (or equivalent)
  • Strong academic record in literary studies

Language Requirements

  • TOEFL iBT: 90 or IELTS: 7.0 (for non-native English speakers)
  • English proficiency may be demonstrated through other standardized tests

Additional requirements may apply for international students. Contact admissions for detailed information.
