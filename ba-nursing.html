Course Overview

The Bachelor of Science in Nursing provides comprehensive clinical and theoretical education to prepare graduates for professional nursing practice in hospitals, clinics and community settings.

Learning Outcomes

📖

Deliver safe and effective nursing care informed by evidence

✍️

Communicate professionally with patients and teams

🔍

Apply critical thinking and clinical decision-making

Course Structure

Year 1 — Foundations
Basic sciences and introductory clinical skills
  • Human Anatomy & Physiology
  • Introduction to Nursing Practice
  • Fundamentals of Patient Care
Year 2 — Clinical Skills
Core clinical practice and patient management
  • Adult Nursing
  • Maternal & Child Health
  • Clinical Practicum
Year 3 — Advanced Practice
Specialist modules and research methods
  • Critical Care Nursing
  • Mental Health Nursing
  • Research Methods
Year 4 — Capstone & Transition
Independent project and professional readiness
  • Capstone Project
  • Advanced Clinical Placement
  • Professional Development

Career Opportunities

Clinical Practice

Registered nurse in hospitals and clinics

Community Health

Public health nurse, community outreach

Leadership & Education

Nurse educator, supervisor

Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 (or equivalent)

Language Requirements

  • TOEFL iBT: 80 or IELTS: 6.5 (for non-native English speakers)
