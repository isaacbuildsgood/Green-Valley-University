Course Overview

The Bachelor of Arts in Theatre Arts provides comprehensive training in performance, design, directing, and dramaturgy. Students develop practical skills through hands-on experience in productions while gaining theoretical knowledge of theatre history, criticism, and contemporary practice.

The program's purpose is to equip students with the technical, artistic, and intellectual tools necessary to succeed as theatre professionals, directors, designers, or scholars. It fosters creativity, collaboration, and a deep understanding of theatre's role in society while preparing graduates for diverse careers in performance and creative industries.

Learning Outcomes

🎭

Develop strong performance skills in acting, voice, and movement

✍️

Analyze dramatic literature and understand theatre history across cultures and periods

🎨

Apply design principles to create theatrical environments through sets, lighting, and costumes

🎬

Develop directing concepts and manage collaborative production processes

🤝

Work effectively in ensemble settings and diverse creative teams

💼

Prepare for professional careers in theatre, film, television, and related industries

Course Structure

Year 1 — Foundations
Core skills and introductory theatre studies
  • Acting Fundamentals
  • Voice and Movement Basics
  • Introduction to Theatre History
  • Design Fundamentals
  • Dramatic Literature Survey
Year 2 — Intermediate Training
Advanced technique and production involvement
  • Acting and Scene Study
  • Advanced Voice and Movement
  • Theatre History: Classical to Modern
  • Lighting and Sound Design
  • Stage Management and Production
Year 3 — Advanced Studies
Specializations and focused training
  • Advanced Acting or Directing
  • Contemporary Theatre Practice
  • Theatre Criticism and Theory
  • Costume and Set Design
  • Electives in Specialization Area
Year 4 — Capstone & Professional
Senior project and career preparation
  • Senior Thesis/Capstone Project
  • Internship or Professional Practicum
  • Advanced Seminar in Theatre
  • Portfolio Development
  • Professional Skills Workshop

Career Opportunities

Professional Performance

Actor, dancer, musician, performer in theatre and film

Design and Technical

Designer, technical director, stage manager, lighting technician

Directing and Dramaturgy

Director, artistic director, dramaturg, playwright

Arts Administration

Theatre manager, producer, arts administrator, development director

Further Study Options

Graduates may pursue Master's or PhD programs in Theatre Arts, as well as related fields including Film Studies, Creative Writing, and Arts Administration.

Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 (or equivalent)
  • Portfolio or audition demonstrating artistic interest (recommended)

Language Requirements

  • TOEFL iBT: 80 or IELTS: 6.5 (for non-native English speakers)
  • English proficiency may be demonstrated through other standardized tests

Additional requirements may apply for international students. Contact admissions for detailed information.
