Course Overview

The Bachelor of Legal Studies covers key areas of law including constitutional, criminal and civil law, equipping students with legal research, argumentation and analytical skills.

Learning Outcomes

📖

Understand foundational legal principles

✍️

Conduct legal research and prepare legal arguments

🔍

Apply ethical considerations in legal contexts

Course Structure

Year 1 — Foundations
Introduction to law and legal methods
  • Introduction to Legal Systems
  • Legal Research Methods
  • Constitutional Law Basics
Year 2 — Core Subjects
Criminal, civil and administrative law
  • Criminal Law
  • Civil Procedure
  • Property Law
Year 3 — Applied Learning
Clinics, internships and electives
  • Legal Clinic
  • Internship Placement
  • Elective Modules
Year 4 — Capstone
Research project and career preparation
  • Capstone Research Project
  • Professional Skills Workshop
  • Career Preparation

Career Opportunities

Legal Support

Paralegal, legal assistant

Policy & Advocacy

Policy advisor, NGO roles

Further Study

Professional law qualifications and postgraduate study

Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 (or equivalent)

Language Requirements

  • TOEFL iBT: 80 or IELTS: 6.5 (for non-native English speakers)
