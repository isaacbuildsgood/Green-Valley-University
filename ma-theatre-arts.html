Course Overview

The Master of Arts in Theatre Arts offers advanced study in theatre history, criticism, directing, and creative practice. Students engage deeply with contemporary and classical theatre, developing expertise in specialized areas while honing their artistic and scholarly skills.

The program's purpose is to prepare students for advanced artistic, academic, and professional careers in theatre and performing arts. It emphasizes independent creative work, critical analysis, and the application of contemporary practices to theatrical production and scholarship.

Learning Outcomes

🎭

Conduct advanced creative work in performance, design, or directing

📚

Analyze theatre history, theory, and dramatic literature critically

💬

Engage in scholarly discourse and artistic collaboration

🎨

Develop expertise in specialized theatrical disciplines

📝

Produce publishable-quality artistic and critical work

🏫

Prepare for doctoral studies or professional artistic leadership roles

Course Structure

Year 1 — Core & Research Methods
Advanced coursework and creative foundations
  • Advanced Theatre History and Criticism
  • Directing and Creative Practice
  • Seminar in Dramatic Literature
  • Contemporary Theatre Performance
  • Research Methodology in Theatre Arts
Year 2 — Specialization & Thesis
Focused creative work and thesis preparation
  • Elective Seminars (choose 2)
  • Independent Creative Project
  • Thesis Research and Writing
  • Professional Development Seminar
  • Artistic Portfolio Preparation

Career Opportunities

Theatre and Performance

Professional director, artistic director, actor, performer

Academic Careers

University teaching, research positions, drama department leadership

Design and Production

Director of design, theatrical producer, technical specialist

Arts Administration

Theatre manager, artistic consultant, cultural institution director

Further Study Options

Graduates may pursue PhD programs in Theatre Arts or related disciplines.

Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Theatre Arts, Drama, or related field
  • Minimum GPA of 3.5 (or equivalent)
  • Strong portfolio or evidence of artistic accomplishment

Language Requirements

  • TOEFL iBT: 90 or IELTS: 7.0 (for non-native English speakers)
  • English proficiency may be demonstrated through other standardized tests

Additional requirements may apply for international students. Contact admissions for detailed information.
