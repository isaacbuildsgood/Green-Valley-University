Course Overview

The Bachelor of Science in Public Health provides foundational knowledge in population health, epidemiology, health promotion and disease prevention, preparing graduates for roles in community health, policy support and health education.

Learning Outcomes

📖

Understand determinants of population health

✍️

Design community health interventions

🔍

Apply basic epidemiological and biostatistical methods

Course Structure

Year 1 — Foundations
Core public health concepts and introductory methods
  • Introduction to Public Health
  • Biology for Public Health
  • Community Health
Year 2 — Methods & Fieldwork
Practical skills and analytics
  • Epidemiology
  • Biostatistics
  • Field Practicum
Year 3 — Specialization
Electives and applied projects
  • Health Promotion
  • Program Planning
  • Electives
Year 4 — Capstone
Independent project and professional preparation
  • Capstone Project
  • Internship/Placement
  • Professional Development

Career Opportunities

Community Health

Health educator, program coordinator

Policy & Research

Analyst, research assistant

NGOs & International Agencies

Program officer, field coordinator

Entry Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0 (or equivalent)

Language Requirements

  • TOEFL iBT: 80 or IELTS: 6.5 (for non-native English speakers)
